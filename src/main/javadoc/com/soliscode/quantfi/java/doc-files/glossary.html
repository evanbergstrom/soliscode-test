In-The-Money An in-the-money option is an option contract that has intrinsic value. It means that the option's strike price (the predetermined price at which the underlying asset can be bought or sold) is favorable compared to the current market price of the underlying asset. For call options, an in-the-money option has a strike price below the market price of the underlying asset, while for put options, the strike price is above the market price. In both cases, exercising the option would result in a profit if done immediately. In-The-Money An out-of-the-money option is an option contract that currently has no intrinsic value. It means that the option's strike price is not favorable compared to the current market price of the underlying asset. For call options, an out-of-the-money option has a strike price above the market price of the underlying asset, while for put options, the strike price is below the market price. If the option is out of the money at expiration, it would not be profitable to exercise the option. However, out-of-the-money options can still have time value and speculative value, depending on various factors such as the time remaining until expiration and market expectations.
